Hello! It’s very nice to meet you.

I’m Roshni Aparna, and my story starts where drawings meet people. Years of Bharatanatyam (100+ stage performances) taught me rhythm, rigor, and how to hold a room—skills I carried to job sites as a Project Coordinator/Assistant Designer, moving RFIs, submittals, takeoffs, cost reviews, procurement, and vendor coordination along a steady beat.

At ASU, I stepped to the other side of the desk as a Graduate Teaching Assistant in Estimation and AI in Construction, helping students turn rough quantities into defensible numbers and introducing practical AI workflows with tools like Kreo and ALICE. I also serve as IFMA Phoenix Student Chapter President, where I plan events, grow membership, and make sure every meeting leaves with owners, dates, and next steps.

I build with both empathy and evidence: an eye for detail, clear communication, time management, and teamwork. My toolkit is hands-on—Revit, AutoCAD, Bluebeam, Procore, MS Project/365, Primavera—backed by certifications in LEED, Bluebeam, Procore, and Primavera.

Recent work I’m proud of includes a conceptual semiconductor pilot facility (ISO Class 3/4 cleanroom; 130-nm on 300-mm wafers) where I mapped high-purity utilities, redundancy, and safety into a coherent plan. And when the day quiets, I write for ParametricArchitecture.com, translating emerging tools into field-ready ideas. In every setting—studio, classroom, or site—I aim to bring order, momentum, and a little grace to the work we share.
